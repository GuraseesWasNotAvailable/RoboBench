21 Jul 2026Result

Phase 0b published — 750 rollouts, five policies

150 episodes per policy on a uniform 600-step horizon. GR00T N1.7 is the only entrant to beat the from-scratch control (+10.6 ± 5.4). The other three are statistically tied with each other and sit at or below a 52M baseline with no pre-training at all.

21 Jul 2026Method

Scoring moved from milestones to continuous placement progress

The old score averaged three stages, two of which were saturated — every policy lifted the block, almost none placed it — leaving all five inside a 9.4-point band. Scoring the block's actual distance to the target, log-scaled, widened that to 13.4 points and cut the error margins from ±13 to ±3.

21 Jul 2026Method

WALL-OSS re-run after an unequal-horizon defect

Its earlier episodes ran 450 steps against 600 for every other policy — 25% less time on a task whose failure mode is running out of clock. Re-run at the common horizon it reached 0.7% success where it had scored 0%. Horizon is now asserted per policy and checked automatically at aggregation.

Five policies entered: GR00T, π0.5, WALL-OSS, SmolVLA, ACT

Selected by popularity across GitHub stars, Hugging Face downloads and research adoption, plus a from-scratch ACT control. All adapted with the same 216 demonstrations and the same step budget.

What's next

4

More task categories

Phase 0 is one pick-and-place task. Four further categories are designed and awaiting scenes — a single task cannot support a general capability claim.

±3

Tighter margins

At 150 episodes we resolve gaps of about 4 points. The three tied models need either more episodes or a more discriminative task to separate.

1

The stopping problem

GR00T locates the target best and then carries the block back out again. A halt-when-close rule is the cheapest experiment on the list.

RoboBench is an independent benchmark. Results are simulation-only and carry no claim about real-robot transfer. Every score here comes from logged, per-episode rollouts.
